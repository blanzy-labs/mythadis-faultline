## Faultline Report

### Faultline Summary

The concept depends on small businesses recognizing the value of structured
pre-build analysis and trusting AI-generated challenges enough to change their
plans.

### Surface Claim

A local-first AI review tool can help small businesses avoid wasting money on
weak product ideas.

### Hidden Assumptions

- Small-business owners will pause to validate ideas before building.
- The target buyer sees failed product work as a costly, recurring problem.
- A structured AI review is credible without saved history or an account.

### Pressure Points

- The buyer and purchase trigger are not yet specific.
- The product must produce useful specificity from uneven user input.
- Provider costs may conflict with a low-price small-business offering.

### Collapse Risks

- Prospects view the report as generic advice they can get elsewhere.
- Customer acquisition costs exceed the value of a one-off review.
- Users mistake the analysis for factual verification and lose trust.

### Weak Evidence

- No paid-intent test has established willingness to pay.
- No interviews demonstrate that pre-build risk review is a priority.
- No comparison shows that two AI passes outperform a simpler checklist.

### What Would Break This

- Owners consistently prefer action over structured validation.
- Reports do not reveal risks users consider new or useful.
- The product cannot support a sustainable margin at an acceptable price.

### Validation Tests

- Interview ten owners who recently spent money on an unsuccessful product idea.
- Offer a manual paid review to five qualified prospects before automating more.
- Compare a two-pass report with a concise human-designed checklist.

### Questions Before Commitment

- Who is the first buyer, and what event makes the review urgent?
- What measurable decision should change after reading a report?
- What price demonstrates meaningful intent without creating a support burden?

### Risk Level

high

### Recommended Next Move

Run a narrow paid manual pilot with a clearly defined buyer before expanding
the product.

## Second-AI Audit Review

### Audit Summary

The scanner identifies demand and differentiation risks but understates the
trust burden and the difficulty of proving that the report changed a decision.

### Missed Risks

- Buyers may require domain expertise that a general-purpose workflow cannot provide.
- Local-first positioning may matter less than speed, price, or confidence.
- The founder may spend too much time explaining reports to support a low-cost product.

### Weak or Vague Findings

- "Useful specificity" needs a measurable quality threshold.
- "Sustainable margin" should include expected provider and support costs.

### Validation Plan Gaps

- The pilot should test whether users act differently after receiving a report.
- The plan needs a retention or repeat-use hypothesis, not only first purchase.

### Risk Level Challenge

High is appropriate until paid demand and decision impact are demonstrated.

### Recommended Report Improvements

- Define a first customer profile and purchase trigger.
- Add a report-quality rubric evaluated by target users.
- Add a minimum price and maximum support-time threshold.

### Final Caution

the report changes a real product decision.
